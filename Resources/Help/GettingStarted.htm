Getting Started

 

To access JCATS click on the Jcats icon on your desktop.  If the icon does not exist then contact your Jcats administrator so they can direct you to the MSI (Microsoft Installer) needed to install Jcats on your computer.

 

You are now ready to Login so you will need a user name and password. If you do not have a user name, contact your system administrator. When you have entered the username and password supplied to you click Go or press the Enter Key.

 

The first time you login you will be taken to the change password screen. Here you will need to enter a password of your choosing. The OLD password is the one that you initially used to access the system. The NEW password is one of your choosing but must comply with password guidelines. The NEW password must be 6 to 20 characters long and contain a minmum of one numeric, one alpha and one non-aplha/numeric character. Special characters such as # or % are examples of non-alpha/numeric characters.

 

Once logged in you will be taken to your appropriate home page. For some of you it will be the search for case page and for others it will be you�re MyJcats page.

 

General Terminology

 

The JCATS system is set up to be navigated in a variety of ways but the primary method is through the use of what are called Navigation Bars. The navigation bars are located in the upper bar of the frame and are labeled Data Entry, Inquiry, Administration, Tasks and Help. Throughout this document, when Navigation is mentioned it is referring to these items. Not every one will see the same entries in the Navigation Bars. The Security Group you belong to will determine the items you will have access to.

 

The Data Entry bar will not be active unless you have retrieved a case. That is because all Data Entry items are case specific. This is important to remember.

 

The items located in the lower bar of the frame are referred to as buttons. After updating a page of data, clicking one of these buttons will cause the updates to be sent back to the data base. As you progress in this book you will be instructed as to which buttons are used for what purposes. In the meantime, suffice it to say that you understand what the term button means.

 

One of the buttons will always be able to be activated by your computers Enter key. This button is clearly identified by a yellow square right next to it. If there are several buttons on a page, each will also have one letter underlined. A combination of the Alt key and that letter will also activate the button. There are other short cuts available as well. For information on those refer to the Short Cuts section of this Help documentation.

 

Another way to move about the system is via Hyper Links. Any blue lettering will be one of those links and take you to a different place in the system.

 

Sub Menus are also used within JCATS and are series of page executions that allow you to accomplish a complete task. For instance a sub menu is in play when you add a new case. By hovering over the sub menu name located in the upper right hand corner, you will see all of the pages required to complete a task such as Case Opening. You will also note that these pages in the sub menu have blue letters, which you already know means they are links. It is not always necessary to complete all steps of a process so this will provide you another form of structured navigation.

 

 

Status Bar is what we call the area at the top of the page that contains information pertinent to the case you are working on whether you are entering a new case or working on an existing case. The Status Bar contains the case name, case #, appointment date, case status, current attorney, and JCATS #.

 

This information remains at the top no matter where you are in the case. You will notice the JCATS # is a blue link. At anytime you may click this link and return to the Main Page of your case.

 

Locating an Existing Case

 

General Search:

 

Inquiry Navigation � Locate and click �Search For Case�

 

JCATS will return a Search Criteria page. You may fill in one or all of the available fields in order to search the database.  Using only the last name criteria is not recommended (unless it is an uncommon name), because it may return a very long list of names.  The more fields that are filled in the more specific the search will be.


Advanced Search:

 

Inquiry Navigation � Locate and click �Advanced Search for Case�

 

The system returns a search criteria page that contains many of the data elements that are found in a case. This is used primarily when you are searching for a group of cases that contain similar data or cases that would have occurred in a particular date range.

 

The same rules apply, the more fields that are filled in the more precise the search will be and the faster it will be.

 

Both search results will be a list of cases matching your search criteria. Clicking the blue link Jcats # will bring the case to your screen. The display will be what is referred to as the Main Page of the case. This page is the hub of the case. You will note a small Icon next to People in Case and Hearings in Case. Only those people currently on the case will be displayed and only hearings that are not resulted are displayed. By clicking the Icon you will display all people ever associated with the case and/or all Hearings held on the case even if they have been resulted. Clicking the Icon again returns the display to its original state.

 

Case Opening

 

Task Navigation � Locate and click on �Add Appointment Case� or on 'Quick Add Case'.

The Case # is the court's case/petition #.

 

Appointment Date will be filled with today�s date. The date is highlighted indicating it is the active field and you may change the date if needed.

 

The Panel check box is used if you desire to enter cases and track them even when they are assigned to independent attorneys.

 

Department is the court room in which the hearings pertaining to this case will be heard.

Note: Upon processing this page, if the Case # already exists in the agency, then Jcats will redirect you to the Main Page of that case. 

 

Adding Children - If the children are your client then check the Client box. The name and gender are the only required fields however it is recommended you enter as much information as possible. You will note that there is a check box with the label �SS�. Check this box to indicate Spanish Speaking.

 

You may add up to 5 children at one time. If there are more than 5 children, simply use the button at the bottom of the screen labeled �Save/Add More� if there are 5 or fewer click �Save/Continue�.  By pressing either of the buttons you will initiate a search of the data base for the names you have entered. The names are processed one at a time. If the name or similar name is found in the data base, that name will be displayed and you will be given the option to copy that name and its demographics to your case �add to case�.  At this point you can copy the information if you feel it is the same person or click the button at the bottom that says �Add As New� indicating you do not feel this is the same person as any of those returned. If you decide you errored and this child should not be added then choose the �Discard� button at the bottom of the page.

 

If the name is not found you will receive �no records were found matching the criteria� and you may either �Add as New� or �Discard�.

 

 

Adding Parents � If one of the Parents is your client then check the box in front of their name. Adding parents works the same way as adding the children. There is an additional field however; the Role is the relationship of the adult to one or more of the children and is a required field. When are finished click �Save/Continue�.

 

 

Add Legal Parties � This section will be used to add the primary people involved in the case such as the Judge, the attorney representing your client, the attorney representing the child if you are representing an adult, the attorney for the adult(s) if you represent the child etc. Their names should be available to you in the dropdowns provided by clicking the arrow at the right of the box to be filled in. At least one agency attorney is required to proceed.

 

You may also enter other people who have roles on the case such as caretaker, additional social workers and many more. The various roles you can enter are located in the dropdown box labeled Role at the bottom of the page which will be empty until you make your entry. Select the Role and enter the pertinent Name and start date information. If you have additional people you wish to enter click �Save/Add More�. When you have added all the people click �Save/Continue�.

 

 

Petition Edit � The next page is the Petition Edit Page. You will fill in the Petition Type from the dropdown that is displayed when you click the dropdown arrow, the file date and the petition number.

 

Directly beneath this information is a list of all of the people you have added to the case. There is a check box next to each name. Check the box for each person that will be associated with this petition (there can be several petitions associated with a case and not everyone is on all of the petitions). At least one parent, one child and one attorney must be selected for each petition.

 

If you have more than one petition to enter click the �New� button at the bottom of the page to add another or click �Save� to continue.

 

Petition Allegation � As many allegations as necessary may be added to each Petition. They are added 4 at a time. If you have more than 4 click the �Add More� button; when you are finished click �Save� to go to the next page.

 

Petition List - shows all of the petitions which have been entered on your case. Clicking the name will take you to that petition for editing or deletion if you feel you have made a mistake. If you are missing a petition click the �Add� button at the bottom to return to the Petition Edit page. When you are satisfied with the list click �Continue�.

 

Associations - This is where you associate the people in the case to each other. Only one association at a time can be entered. You will either �Save/Add More� or �Save/Continue�.

 

AKAs - AKA stands for Also Known AS and are aliases that may be used by one or more of the people. Again you have the option to �Save/Add More� or �Save/Continue�.

 

Legal Numbers - This is where you add any other legal numbers on a case. These are not required fields.

 

Address/Placement - The page displays the ability to enter an address, use an address that has already been entered or use a system address for an existing Institution or Placement facility. Once an address has been entered it moves into the Existing Address dropdown. This eliminates the need to enter the same address for multiple people multiple times. The address therefore only needs to be entered once and can be used over and over. If the address requires correcting it only needs to be corrected once no matter how many people it is associated with.

 

The people on the case are also displayed. When you enter an address, you find the person the address is for, select from the �Type� dropdown for that person what kind of address it is (Home, Work, etc.), enter a start date and click the button �Save/Add More� until all of your people have addresses or at least the ones you want to have addresses. When you are finished you click �Save/Continue�.

 

You will notice there is also a �Save/Print� button if you desire to produce a hard copy of the addresses.

 

Hearings -The page is in three sections; Add Hearing, Petitions in Case and Hearings In Case. The Add Hearing Section has a Type field which is a dropdown window. Hearing Types are preloaded in the system. If you should encounter a situation where the hearing type you need is not in the dropdown, contact your system administrator to have the hearing type added.

 

Select the proper hearing type from the dropdown, hearing date, hearing time, the Judicial Officer and the department. Hearing officers and departments are also preloaded in the system and selected from a dropdown.

If the hearing date is not in the future then you can also use this page to record the attorney's in-court time by selecting the Attending Attorney and populating the Hours Worked fields.  Upon entering the Hours Worked (use tenths of hours) the Work Description and Phase values are automatically displayed in their respective dropdowns.  If there is more than one option in the dropdown then the user must select the appropriate value.  If there is only one option then Jcats automatically selects it.

 

The Petitions in Case section is where you associate the hearing you entered with the appropriate petitions. Click the check box net to the petitions this hearing will apply to. When you are finished click the button Save/Add if you have more hearings to add. Alist of all current hearings will appear at the bottom of the screen under the section titled Hearings in Case. If you have no more hearings to enter, click the button Save/Continue to move to the next page.

 

A Hearing Note section is available if there is anything you wish to note concerning this Hearing. The note section is free form and JCATS does use a spell checker.

 

Note: This is also the page you end up going to when you result a hearing. Each petition may have its own result or there may be a Global result for all petitions associated with that particular hearing. This is discussed more thoroughly in the resulting hearings section.

 

Action Requests - Action Requests are generated to instruct an Investigator or Paralegal to perform a specific task relative to this case. The request is automatically forwarded to the Investigator or Paralegal as well as their Supervisor. Action Requests may be one of two formats; a general request accessed by clicking the blue link titled �Add Action Request� or a standard request accessed by clicking the blue link �Add Standard Action Request�. Keyboard shortcuts can also be used here. Using �Alt/A� activates the general request and �Alt/S� the standard request. You can tell this because the A in the general request is underlined and the S in the standard request is underlined.

 

Add Action Request is called the general request type because there are no predefined specific tasks. The task for this request would be detailed in the Notes Request box. This is a free form field and is spell checked. The display returned when you click the link provides the format for filling out the request. Each request can be for an Investigator, an Attorney, a Paralegal or an Intern.

 

Several of the items in the display may already be filled in by default. For instance if an attorney has been assigned they will be the default requester. The date of the request is also defaulted to today�s date and if an Investigator has been assigned they will also be defaulted. Of course all of these may be filled in using the dropdown menus. All dropdown menus have been preloaded in the system by your system administrator.

 

You will need to enter the Request Type, the Hearing the request is associated with and the due date. Then click the client(s) this request applies to and if a visit is required check that box as well. Fill in the specifics of the request in the note field and click the �Add� button.

 

It is not necessary to associate a request with a particular hearing but as a general rule most will be associated with a particular hearing.

 

Add Standard Action Request - A standard request replaces the free form area with predefined tasks. Each task will be selected by clicking the check box in front of the task. The display returned when you click the link provides the format for filling out the request. Each request can be for an Investigator, an Attorney, a Paralegal or an Intern.

 

Several of the items in the display may already be filled in by default. For instance if an attorney has been assigned they will be the default requester. The date of the request is also defaulted to today�s date and if an Investigator has been assigned they will also be defaulted. Of course all of these may be filled in using the dropdown menus. All dropdown menus have been preloaded in the system by your system administrator.

 

You will need to enter the Request Type, the Hearing the request is associated with and the due date. Then click the client(s) this request applies to, click the appropriate Tasks being requested and then click the �Add� button.

 

It is not necessary to associate a request with a particular hearing but as a general rule most will be associated with a particular hearing.

 

Notes - Your system administrator will preload note types for you to select from the Type dropdown menu. Much like an email the subject line is always preferred and is a free form field.

 

Enter the communication you wish to memorialize in the note field. This is a free form field and is spell checked. The page section labeled �Broadcast Notes Display Pages� provides a list of pages you can broadcast the note to. By broadcasting the note an Icon is displayed in the status bar of that page and by clicking the Icon people will readily be able to read the note. The broadcast indicates that an important note has been added that is relevant to that page and will prompt a user to read a note they may otherwise overlook.

 

To Broadcast a note, click the check box in front of each page you want the note broadcasted on.
